Being a part of EMCDE…GeoDsc


I wanted to study more, much more than a bachelor in engineering. I was in a good place, having a good job, learning many things, growing my network but in this big bright career, I always felt a small tiny dark space. This gloomy void kept reminding me that I needed to study more, that I needed to progress academically, and that if I did, I would be more confident.

I was working as a research associate in the Geoinformatics Center (GIC) of the Asian Institute of Technology since 4 February 2019. The centre has great expertise in the field of remote sensing and GIS. Initially, I was recruited as a part of a project "Implementation of Integrated Geospatial Platform, Database, and Applications for Disaster Risk Management in Uttarakhand " funded by the World Bank. Followed by this I worked in several project for GIC until 15 August 2020. It seems to be a small period but trust me, I learned as much as half of my life. When I say half of my life, it sounds so old, yet I was the youngest at the time I worked there. It was a wonderful time there and I didn't expect to leave the place any soon but I knew to contribute more, develop confidence in myself and make it count for what work I do I have to go for higher studies.

It was Ashok Dahal, who once mentioned to me about Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Program. Before this, I knew nothing about Master in Copernicus Digital Earth and when learned about it I didn’t think I am qualified for this. The course work was vast and covered emerging topics. I was scared that if I got lost in these enormous themes, I wouldn't be able to perform adequately. On top of that, I hadn't prepared for TOFEL and wasn't sure if I wanted to leave GIC so soon. The course looked incredibly interesting, so I decided to give it a shot. I applied for it with Geodata Science specialization. I sincerely hoped to be selected, but I couldn't help but question, "Why would they choose me when I'm not very talented in this area and don't have a high TOFEL score?".

“The Selection Committee has assessed your complete application documents and it’s our pleasure to inform you that you have been selected as Erasmus+ EMJMD scholar for the ‘Copernicus Master in Digital Earth’ programme”. I received this email few weeks later and was delighted to see this message. I was overjoyed, and it wasn't until later that I realized my interview had gone well, and I believe they thought I deserved it. It is great to know that they have faith in me, and it gives me the courage to start afresh and give my best to every task I am assigned.

Despite the fact that the story is over a year ago, I can still remember how happy I was when I received the email. Today, here I am, learnt a lot of new things, met a lot of new people, and I feel like I've gained a lot of confidence in myself. Academically and psychologically, I have grown. At this moment, I can confidently assert to myself that I am capable. And I'm all set to begin my specialized track.

My message to everyone reading this and wondering to choose whether or not pursue CDE is: if you genuinely love this domain and are ready of handling innovative geospatial work, then jump in and give it your all. Be down to earth, yet monitoring from up.


Thank you for your time and patience. Be good do good.
